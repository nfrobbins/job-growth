Idaho and Nevada lead the nation in job growth

by Natalie Robbins

December 11, 2023


Over the last year, jobs grew in 48 states, with the highest rates of growth in Idaho and Nevada. The percent change of people employed in non-farm jobs increased in every state except for Mississippi, where it stayed the same, and Rhode Island, where it decreased by half a percent.

Idaho seems to be the place to be for potential job seekers. Idaho leads the nation in job growth from October 2022 to 2023, with a 3.5 percent increase in non-farm jobs. Idaho ranks 21st in national unemployment, with an unemployment rate of 3.2 percent. The national unemployment rate in October 2023 was 3.9 percent.

Nevada has the highest unemployment rate in the country at 5.4 percent. But perhaps things are looking up for Nevadans: the state had the second-highest rate of job growth at 3.4 percent.

In an interview with The Nevada Current, Nevada Department of Employment's chief economist David Schmidt attributes the high unemployment rates to the housing bust of the Great Recession, from which the state still struggles to recover.

Schmidt says job growth can be accounted for by the fact that in Las Vegas, Nevada employs many people in the hospitality industry, which is growing for the first time since the pandemic.

He calls the high unemployment rate combined with high job growth rates a "weird paradox."
